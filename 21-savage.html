21 Savage (Rapper) Height, Weight, Age, Net worth, Biography &amp; More

Publish date: 2024-02-08

21 Savage (born October 22, 1992) is a British-born American Rapper, Record Producer, Songwriter, and Social Media Star from Plaistow, London. He initially gained success in the music industry after releasing his debut mixtape “The Slaughter Tape“. His real full birth name is Shéyaa Bin Abraham-Joseph.

The Slaughter Tape came out on May 25, 2015. Earlier, he released his first single “Picky” on November 12, 2014. Furthermore, Shéyaa signed a deal with Sonny Digital and published an Extended Play “Free Guwop” on July 02, 2015.

The rapper attained nationwide fame after collaborating with Metro Boomin (producer and record executive). They released a super hit EP called “Savage Mode” in 2016.

Songs List, Music Album, Career & Net worth

Their songs like “X” and “No Heart” became very popular. In fact, Joseph also featured Drake’s single track “Sneakin” in the project. On July 07, 2017, Savage came up with his debut studio album “Issa Album“.

It was a commercially successful project which also topped at the second position on the Billboard 200. The single track “Bank Account” spotted on the Billboard Hot 100 top 20 song.

Some of his other popular works are Rockstar, Without Warning, I Am > I Was, and much more. As of wealth, the talented 21 Savage’s Net worth is $8 million US Dollars, as of 2019.

Early Life, Parents, Siblings & Profile

Biography: Rapper 21 Savage, was born on the 22nd of October 1996 (age 26 years old, as of 2019) in Plaistow, London, England. His parents are Kevin Cornelius Emmons (father) and Heather Carmillia Joseph (mother).

He belongs to multiracial ethnicity and is of Dominica and Haitian descent. He has two siblings i.e. twin-sister named Kyra and Jayda Davis. They worked as an American dance choreographer.

At the age of 7, his mom and dad divorced each other and he moved to Atlanta with Heather. Shéyaa Bin used to attend DeKalb County School District. But when he was in the 7th grade, the school banned for gun possession.

The rapper also sent to the detention center and served some time there. Later, he tried to continue his education and pursued it through alternative courses. However, Savage dropped out later and joined a local street gang named Bloods street gang.
