Joey King Bio, Age, Parents, Husband, Children, Net Worth – Joey King is a highly talented American actress whose career has spanned various genres and mediums, earning her acclaim and recognition in the entertainment industry.

Joey King Biography

Born on July 30, 1999, she initially rose to prominence by portraying the endearing Ramona Quimby in the comedy film “Ramona and Beezus” in 2010. However, it was her leading role in the romantic comedy “The Kissing Booth” and its subsequent sequels in 2018, 2020, and 2021 that catapulted her to even greater fame.

One of Joey’s career highlights includes her remarkable performance in the crime drama series “The Act” in 2019. Her portrayal of Gypsy Rose Blanchard garnered critical acclaim and earned her prestigious award nominations, such as the Primetime Emmy Award and Golden Globe Award.

Joey’s journey in the entertainment world began early, as she attended Phoenix Ranch School in Simi Valley. Even as a child, her talent shone through, with performances that included a cappella singing at the Simi Valley Cultural Arts Center and acting with the Stage Door Children’s Theatre in Agoura.

Apart from her successful acting career, Joey King comes from a family with a strong presence in the industry. She has two older sisters, Kelli King and Hunter King, who are also accomplished actresses. While Joey acknowledges her Jewish heritage, she describes herself as not particularly religious.

Joey King’s career timeline includes voice acting in “Horton Hears a Who!” (2008) and appearances in various television series such as “Ghost Whisperer,” “The Suite Life of Zack & Cody,” “Entourage,” “CSI: Crime Scene Investigation,” and “Life in Pieces.” Her first leading role came in 2010 when she portrayed Ramona Quimby, a performance that earned her a Young Artist Award.

Over the years, Joey has showcased her versatility by taking on diverse roles, including parts in “The Dark Knight Rises” (2012), “Crazy, Stupid, Love” (2011), and Taylor Swift’s “Mean” music video. Her filmography expanded with appearances in “The Possibility of Fireflies” (2016), “Wish Upon” (2017), “The Kissing Booth” series, and “The Act.”

Beyond her acting career, Joey King has ventured into producing television content, signing deals with both Hulu and Netflix. She remains a prominent and promising talent in the entertainment industry, with exciting projects like “A Family Affair” on Netflix set for release in 2024.
