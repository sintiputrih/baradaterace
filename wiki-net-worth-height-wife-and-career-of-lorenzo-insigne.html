How did Lorenzo Insigne start his Professional Career?

Insigne joined Caves 1919 in February 2010 and made his professional league debut in the Lega Pro Prima Divisione of the Italian Professional League.

He was sold to Foggia in 2010 as part of a co-ownership deal with Napoli, where he scored 19 goals while playing for Czech coach Zdenek Zeman.

Insigne returned to Zeman on July 8, 2011, having accepted a one-year loan from Serie B team Pescara. With 18 goals and 14 assists that season, he was the club’s second-highest scorer in the league, making a significant contribution to the team’s triumph.

After winning Serie B, Pescara has been promoted to Serie A thanks to Zeman’s ultra-attacking style of play.

Due to his performance at Pescara, Insigne’s parent club Napoli decided to bring him back for the 2012–13 campaign because they saw him as a long-term replacement for the recently departed Ezequiel Lavezzi.

He scored his first Serie A goal for Napoli on September 16, 2012, during a 3-1 victory over Parma at home. This season, he played in 43 games and contributed seven assists along with five goals.

Insigne frequently started games on the bench because of competition for starting spots from players like Edinson Cavani, Goran Pandev, Eduardo Vargas, and Omar El Kaddouri.
