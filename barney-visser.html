Barney Visser is an entrepreneur and businessman most known for being not only a part-owner of Furniture Row but also the owner of Furniture Row Racing, a winning race team that participates in the NASCAR Monster Energy Cup Series. He is also a  novelist and producer.

Barney Visser was born in Denver, Colorado, USA in 1949. The precise date of birth is unclear to the media. His career dates back to the mid-1970s. Other sources include his work as an author and as a producer.

Barney Visser grew up in his hometown and attended Denver Christian High School for three years before transferring to Thomas Jefferson High School.

How did Barney Visser start his Professional Career?

Following that, he shifted his focus to the business world, and in the early 1970s, Barney began to produce huge poof pillows, founding his furniture store firm called Furniture Row.

In 1977, as his business expanded, he added eight “Pillow Kingdom” boutiques. He quickly expanded his production by making waterbed pillows, which he sold in the business “Big Sur Waterbeds.”

As Barney’s company gained significant financial success, this represented the beginning of an increase in his net worth. His company had 85 stores in total during the 1980s, and now it has over 330 outlets in more than 30 states, which has significantly increased its net worth.

Aside from his financial pursuits, Barney is well-known for his involvement in the sports sector, which he began as a racer at Colorado National Speedway.

There he met Jerry Robertson, with whom he co-founded Furniture Row Racing, a NASCAR Busch Series (now Nationwide Series) team that participates in the Monster Energy NASCAR Cup Series, in 2005.

They are based in Denver and are represented by drivers Erik Jones and Martin Truex, Jr., which has impacted his net worth. To expand on his career, Barney is also an author, having released a book about his experiences in the Vietnam War in 2000 titled “Vietnam: Fresh, Positive Insights For All Who Suffered Loss In The Vietnam War.”

Aside from that, he worked as an executive producer on the 2000 film “Bored Silly” and the 2003 film “Uncle Nino,” which increased his net worth.
