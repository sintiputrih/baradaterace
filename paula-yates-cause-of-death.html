Publish date: 2024-05-03

Paula Yates Cause Of Death: Paula Yates, officially known as Paula Elizabeth Yates was a British television presenter and writer born on April 24, 1959.

In 1979, Yates began her career as a music journalist with a column called “Natural Blonde” in the Record Mirror, shortly after posing for Penthouse magazine

She made her TV debut on Channel 4 on November 5 1982 on the music program The Tube as co-presenter (with Jools Holland). Yates was also known for her presenting roles on The Big Breakfast.

Paula Yates attracted a lot of attention as a result of her marriage to singer, Bob Geldof and her relationship with singer, Michael Hutchence.

She appeared alongside her friend Jennifer Saunders in 1987 for a spoof documentary on the pop group Bananarama. She died on September 17, 2000.

In 2023, her name popped up again after it was disclosed that, the Life of the late TV presenter will be the focus of a new two-part documentary.

The Channel 4 program will center on four “extraordinarily compelling” previously-unheard interviews with Yates, which were recorded in 1998 and 1999.

A boss at Channel 4, Shaminder Nahal said: “Paula Yates exploded onto our screens in the very first week that Channel 4 came on air in 1982, a whirlwind of wit, verve and charisma – a totally unique style.

“Looking at what she achieved now, it feels like no one has ever quite matched her as a TV presenter. So as Channel 4 reflects on 40 years, it feels right to look at her life and career, and what an impact she made.”

“As ever, (documentary producers) Curious Films has made a riveting and sensitive series that will, I hope, introduce Paula to a new generation.”

The documentary will also include testimonies from close friends and former colleagues of Yates’s, as well as footage from a number of other interviews and the programmes she presented.
