Ann Curry | News Report, Investments & Books

News Report

Curry became a reporter and anchor for NBC station KGW in Portland in 1980. She relocated to Los Angeles four years later to work as a reporter for KCBS-TV, where she won two Emmy Awards between 1984 and 1990.

Further, Ann joined NBC News in 1990, initially as a Chicago correspondent for the network, then as the host of NBC News at Sunrise from 1991 until 1996.

Similarly, she worked as a news anchor at Today from 1997 until 2011. She became the show’s second-longest serving news anchor, behind Frank Blair, who worked there from 1953 to 1975.

Curry was chosen co-host of Dateline NBC alongside Stone Phillips on June 24, 2005; after Phillips left on July 2, 2007, she served as the lead anchor until she replaced Meredith Vieira on Today in 2011.

Similarly, the hardworking TV journalist became a national and international correspondent-anchor for NBC News in June 2012 and the host of the Today show’s “Ask the Anchor” segment.

Further, she has reported on major international stories, filing stories from Baghdad, Sri Lanka, Congo, Rwanda, Albania, and Darfur.

Ann Curry | Career

Ann Curry started her broadcasting career in 1978 as an intern at KTVL in Medford, Oregon, which was then an NBC affiliate (and is now a CBS affiliate).

She progressed through the station ranks to become the first female news reporter.

Meredith Vieira was promoted to the top job in 2006, and Ann was passed over. Nevertheless, the hardworking journalist focused her reporting on breaking foreign events.

Similarly, Curry hosted NBC’s primetime coverage and highlights of the Live Earth concerts on July 7, 2007.

She also contributed with interviews for the special with New York City Mayor Michael Bloomberg and former Vice President Al Gore.

Ann announced her departure from NBC News on January 13, 2015, after nearly 25 years.

Ann did TED Talks about journalism in 2018.

Currently, Curry is a virtual journalism instructor at American University in Washington, D.C. Topics covered in her classes include war correspondence, ethics, and believability.
