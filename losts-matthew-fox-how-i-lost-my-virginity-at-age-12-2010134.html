Lost's Matthew Fox: How I Lost My Virginity at Age 12

Publish date: 2024-07-25

How precocious!

Lost star Matthew Fox says he lost his virginity at a rather young age.

"I was 12," Fox, now 43, says in the May issue of Playboy.

And just like his hit ABC show (which wraps up next month), his first sexual experience was filled with adventure and intrigue.

His unnamed lover, he says, "was about two years older than me. It wasn't her first time."

Fox continues: "I can actually see the event in my mind's eye, like photographs. It was in Dubois, Wyoming, where the population sign probably says, to this day, about 1,000."

And he wasn't deflowered in a cozy bedroom, either: "It happened literally on the ground by a river while a rodeo was going on in town," he confessed. "It was absolutely terrible and awkward–just two f***ing kids lying down and pulling our pants down."

The actor, who leads the island survivors as Dr. Jack Shephard, also details his more satisfying erotic experiences as a football player at Columbia University. "My sex escapades at Columbia were in my freshman year," he says. "That's what you're supposed to do. We didn't get any of the benefits of being football heroes. There was plenty of sex but no football-hero sex."

These days, however, he's remained faithful to wife of 19 years Margherita — despite tabloid reports of his affair with a stripper.

"The story is not true, and I'm not going to comment on it," he tells Playboy.

"The subject of fidelity is very personal," Fox adds, and goes on to praise Margharita, mother to his two children.
