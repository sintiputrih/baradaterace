Jon Bones Jones: Career

Early Career

Jon Jones began his career at Iowa Central Community College as a wrestler and defensive lineman.

In addition, he was a state champion in high school and a JUCO champion at Iowa Central Community College.

Further, Jon began his Mixed Martial Arts career in 2008 after finishing college and training to become a professional fighter.

Furthermore, following his debut in April 2008, the MMA fighter went undefeated for three months.

In addition, he won the USKBA Light Heavyweight title in 2008, defeating Moyses Gabin by TKO in the final.

Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC)

After winning the USKBA Light Heavyweight Championship, he also won the Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC).

As a consequence, Jones inked a contract with UFC at the end of July 2008.

Finally, on August 9, 2008, Jon Jones made his octagon debut by fighting against the Andre Gusmao.

Subsequently, Jones was victorious with a unanimous decision in his first-ever UFC fight.

Furthermore, he fought Stephan Bonner at UFC 94 and Jake O’Brien at UFC 100; Jones won by submission in both matches.

Nevertheless, Jones’s first competitive and major fight was on December 5, 2009, against Matt Hamill, Light Heavyweight Champion.

Evidently, Jon Jones was dominating the fight against the champ as they landed some crucial punches that took down Matt.

Unfortunately, despite having dominated the match, Jones was disqualified for using downward elbow strikes.

Afterward, The UFC fighter Jones went on to achieve the Knockout of the Night award after TKO with a cold-blooded elbow to Brandon Vera on March 21, 2010.

Jones started making an impact in UFC after series of wins and notably defeating Ryan Bader who was undefeated at the time.

Meanwhile, Jon was given the nickname the Bones for his astute performance.

Evidently, on March 19, 2011, The Bones became the youngest Light Heavyweight Champion after deafeating Maurício “Shogun” Rua.

Then Began the reign of Jon Bones Jones as the Light Heavyweight Champion as he went on 17 unbeaten titles defending streak.

Unfortunately, he was fired in April 2015, after he hit and ran over a pregnant woman in New York.

Fortunately, after six months of the incident, he was resigned by UFC and made UFC comeback victory against Ovince Saint Preux.

This victory earned him an interim Light Heavyweight championship.

Doping Violation & Suspension

On July 7, Jones recorded a UFC history, but this time, it was for a bad reason as he became the first UFC roaster ever to be stripped of his title twice.

The UFC star was found guilty of using drugs by the U.S. Anti-Doping Agency (USADA).

Subsequently, after facing a 1-year ban from UFC, Jones was banned again from UFC after a doping violation in November 2016.

Hold your horses there! That’s not the end of the doping violation committed by the UFC fighter Jon Jones.

Additionally, Jones was suspended for 15 months after being tested positive for an anabolic steroid in July 2017.

Second reign as UFC Light Heavy Weight champion

Following his 15 months ban, Jones returned to UFC on October 11, 2018.

Nevertheless, The UFC fighter Jon defeated the veteran fighter Alexander Gustafsson to clinch the UFC Light HEavy weight championship.

Not to mention, Jones went on to win series of his title defenses against Anothy Smith, Dominick Reyes, and many more.
