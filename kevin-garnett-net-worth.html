Kevin Garnett | Career

Garnett started his professional NBA career playing for Minnesota Timberwolves.

He played 12 seasons for Minnesota Timberwolves. He averaged 14 points, 8.4 rebounds, and 2.26 blocks in his rookie season.

In 2003-04, Garnett scored his career-high stat with an average of 24.2 points, 13.9 rebounds, five assists, and 2.2 blocks, enough to make him the Most Valuable Player in the league.

Furthermore, in 2007-08, he moved to the Boston Celtics and played for six seasons and also helped the team win NBA championship.

After a successful career with Boston, Garnett moved to Brooklyn Nets in 2013. However, Kevin did not enjoy success at the Nets in his two-season stint.

In 2015, Kevin decided to join his former team Minnesota Timberwolves and played for two seasons. Meanwhile, the homecoming did not go well with his performance.

Aside from that, he has represented the United States and has won the Olympic gold medal at the 2000 Summer Olympics.
