Who Is Ben Foster?

Ben Foster is a British footballer and goalkeeper for Wrexham.

Born on April 3, 1983, in England, Foster began his career with Warwick before he joined Stoke City in 2001.

He later went on to play for Bristol City, Tiverton Town, Stafford Rangers, Kidderminster Harriers and Wrexham.

In 2005, he began playing for Manchester United, appearing in 23 games. He then switched to Birmingham City in 2010, and helped the team win the 2011 League Cup.

Foster moved to West Bromwich Albion on loan and later to Watford and West Brom.

In 2014, Foster was selected to represent England’s at the FIFA World Cup.

Foster made over 500 first-team appearances and won eight England caps.

Foster retired from international football in September 2022, after rejecting an offer from Newcastle United.

In March 2023, Foster came out of retirement, just six months after quitting to rejoin Wrexham, on a short-term deal.
