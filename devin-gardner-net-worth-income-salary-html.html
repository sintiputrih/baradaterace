Devin Gardner (born December 14, 1991) is an American football quarterback who is presently the starting quarterback for the Michigan Wolverines football team in the 2013 NCAA Division I-A football season. In 2013, he finished his redshirt junior season with a 4.0 GPA. The Michigan Football Legends jersey belonging to Tom Harmon was presented to Gardner early in the 2013 season, 

making him the first Michigan player to wear the number in more than seven decades. The first seven games of the 2013 season had seen Gardner amass 1,779 passing yards, 660 running yards, and 22 touchdowns to his credit (13 passing, 9 rushing). Gardner passed John Navarre’s single-game throwing yardage record of 503 yards on October 19, 2013,  to become the first Michigan quarterback to do it. With 584 yards of total offence, he also shattered Denard Robinson’s single-game Michigan record for total offence. Gardner was regarded as the No. 1 dual-threat quarterback in the United States by Rivals.com and as the No. 5 quarterback by ESPNU as a high school senior. He began his college career at the University of Michigan in January 2010 and played in just a few games over the 2010 and 2011 seasons. During the first part of the 2012 season,  he transitioned to the wide receiver position, but when Denard Robinson suffered an elbow injury, he returned to the quarterback position.
